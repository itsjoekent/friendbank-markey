We win when we engage our personal networks as deeply as possible.You are connected to so many potential voters, which means you have the unique power to get them involved. Reaching out personally is the most effective way to engage voters, and in a race as close as this one, it could make the difference.

How to engage your network and help Ed win:

  • Ask each of your friends individually and directly to sign up on your page and get involved.
  • Share your page on social media and write a post asking your network to sign it — and do so more than once. People are more likely to engage with your post if they see it multiple times!
  • Make it personal. Talk about what brings you to the table and why you’re volunteering for Ed — and why it’s important to you that your friends do, too.
  • Go beyond friends and family. Think about all the different types of people you know that you can reach out to: neighbors, colleagues, roommates, classmates, people you talk to on social media… go deep!
  • Set a goal of the number of new signups you want, and stick to it. Then share your goal with your network — ask them to help you reach it.
  • Keep sharing your page — and follow up! Someone has signed up on your page and they’re an Ed supporter, great! Have they committed to volunteering yet? Will they make a page of their own and bring their friends and family into the fold?
